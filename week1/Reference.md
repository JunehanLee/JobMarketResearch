## 1. UK Labour Market Context

### 1.1 ONS Labour market overview

**Source:** Office for National Statistics (ONS)  
**Title:** Labour market overview, UK  
**Link:** [[ONS vacancy bulletin link] ](https://www.ons.gov.uk/employmentandlabourmarket/peopleinwork/employmentandemployeetypes/bulletins/uklabourmarket/april2026) 
**Used for:** Overall UK vacancy trend and macro labour-market context.  
**Relevant evidence:** UK vacancies have declined from the post-pandemic peak, indicating a weaker overall hiring environment.  
**Notes / limitations:** This source is not junior-role-specific. It is used as macro context only.

---

**Source:** Adzuna  
**Title:** UK Job Market Report  
**Used for:** Jobseekers per vacancy, advertised vacancies, advertised salary trends, and category-level hiring pressure.

## 6. Notes on Evidence Quality

The sources above are used at different evidence levels:

| Evidence type | Sources | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Macro labour-market context | ONS vacancy data | Strong for overall market trend, weak for junior-specific conclusions |
| Market competition | Adzuna jobseekers per vacancy | Useful directional indicator |
| Category-level hiring pressure | Adzuna annual-change charts | Useful for identifying pressured categories, not causality |
| Business AI adoption | GOV.UK / ONS | Strong for adoption trends, not direct replacement |
| Individual AI usage | YouGov | Useful for bottom-up AI use signals |
| AI capability progress | Epoch AI | Strong for benchmark progress, not full workplace substitution |
